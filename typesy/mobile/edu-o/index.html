Keyboarding – the most essential computer skill – is now required by the common core.

In 2019, correct touch typing (keyboarding) is the most important technology skill for students to learn. Keyboarding is an essential part of the common core standards, so any student with sub-par keyboarding skills will be behind the these important national standards. More importantly, students who can’t type properly are at a severe disadvantage in computer-based testing, because their bad typing slows them down, wasting valuable test time. Here are just a few of the advantages great keyboarding offers students, teachers, and schools:

Common Core Compliance

keyboarding skills are part of the common core standards. Students with good keyboarding skills can meet and exceed these standards.

Computer-based Testing

Students with fast and accurate typing will do better in computer-based tests, lifting results for the student, and also the entire school.

Better Educational Outcomes

Correct touch typing makes it easier to type assignments, do research, and communicate. Students complete more work and get better results.
